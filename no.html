What can you do then?

It's good to pay attention to the flow of the story, and look for the opportunity to use one of your skills. Take a look at the following list while the DM is describing the setting and see if an opportunity to use one of your skills comes up (you should have two from your class and one or more from your background or race)

Acrobatics

  • Climb a building
  • Escape from something grabbing you
  • Do a backflip

Animal Handling

  • Calm a panicked horse
  • Beckon a cat to come closer
  • Tame a wild hawk

Arcana

  • Figure out the source of a phenomenon's magic
  • Determine the origin of a magic item
  • Actively complete a magical formula

Athletics

  • Suplex a boulder
  • Break down a door
  • Climb a mountain
  • Swim across a river

Deception

  • Pump up the value of an item you intend to sell
  • Convince a local rub that you are actually a vampire, in broad daylight
  • Turn the court against a particular noble with a lie

History

  • Recall knowledge about a region
  • Determine the historical significance of a piece of treasure
  • Remember something important that has happened sometime within the adventure

Insight

  • Determine if a merchant is lying about the authenticity of his goods
  • Decipher the intent behind a gruesome crime
  • Try to see if you can read another character's expression

Intimidation

  • Scare an innkeep into letting you stay the night free
  • Attempt to frighten a flock of birds
  • Make a convincing threat to a rival or enemy

Investigation

  • Gather clues in a crime scene
  • Determine who last used a particular item, and what for
  • Thoroughly observe the carvings of a wall to look for a trap door

Medecine

  • Bandage a gapping wound of an ally or friend
  • Determine the cure for a particular disease
  • Prepare herbs that could be used for medicine

Nature

  • Determine the species of a particular monster
  • See if it's going to rain soon
  • Attempt to predict a particular animal's behaviour

Perception

  • Look for traps
  • Observe any differences in a place you just visited
  • Search for hidden clues or enemies

Performance

  • Attempt to entertain a passing caravan
  • Emulate a sung sonata to acompany an elven bard
  • Provide an eye catching diversion

Persuasion

  • Convince the local barmade for a free drink
  • Debate with a foe into changin their ways
  • Woo a noble to your cause

Religion

  • Recall the origins of a heinous rune
  • Utter an appropriate prayer to the fallen
  • Determine the source of a divine influence

Sleight of Hand

  • Steal a watch from a passing noble
  • Snag a treasure attop a pedestal without triggering a trap
  • Swap a magical item for a fake without getting caught

Stealth

  • Hide in a bush from a passing war band
  • Silently approach a sleeping dragon
  • Avoid the attention of a passing guard

Survival

  • Forage for food in a forest
  • Track an animal or a monster
  • Splint a broken leg without any real tools

Do note that all of these examples begin with a verb. Ability checks are things that you actively participate in. If theres something you want to do as your character, but you don't know what roll is appropriate, ask the DM for what roll would be appropriate. You might be required to have a specific set of tools on hand to perform the action you wish if the DM determines so (eg. if you have no medicine kit to bandage a wound, you would make a survival check instead of a medicine check)
